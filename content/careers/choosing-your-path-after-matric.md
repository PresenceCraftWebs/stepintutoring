## You have more options than you think

Matric is not a dead end if you don't get a university pass — and university
is not the only good path even if you do.

### University (degree)

Needs a **Bachelor's pass**. Apply directly to universities (most open
applications between **April and September** of your matric year). Check each
course's **APS score** requirements early so you know what marks to aim for.

### TVET college (diploma / certificate)

Practical, career-focused programmes — electrician, boilermaker, office
administration, IT support, hospitality. Often cheaper, shorter, and NSFAS
funds these too.

### Learnerships and apprenticeships

Earn while you learn. Companies and SETAs advertise learnerships — you get
workplace experience plus a qualification. Search "SETA learnerships" plus
your field.

### A planned gap year

Only take one if you have a plan: improve your marks through a second-chance
matric programme, work, or volunteer. An aimless year is hard to recover from.

## How to actually decide

1. **Start from subjects you enjoy and do well in** — not from job titles.
2. Talk to someone doing the job. Ask your tutor to connect you.
3. Check the *real* entry requirements this year, not what someone remembers.
4. Have a plan B and a plan C application in — applications are cheap or free;
   missed deadlines are expensive.
