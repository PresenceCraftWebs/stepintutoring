## The one big idea

Reading your notes again *feels* like studying, but research is clear:
**testing yourself** is far more effective. Every hour you spend answering
questions is worth several hours of re-reading.

## A simple weekly system

1. **Watch the lesson once**, making short notes in your own words.
2. **Do the worksheet or past-paper questions** without looking at notes.
3. **Mark your own work** against the memo — mistakes are where learning
   happens.
4. **Come back in a few days** and redo only the questions you got wrong.

## Past papers are your best friend

- The Department of Basic Education publishes past NSC papers with memos for
  free — your tutor can share them, or find them on the DBE website.
- In this app, look for lessons tagged **Exam Prep** and **Past Paper**.
- Time yourself for at least two full papers before the real exam.

## Small things that add up

- Study in short focused blocks (25–40 minutes) with real breaks.
- Phone in another room — or downloaded lessons on flight mode.
- Sleep matters: a tired brain cannot recall what it knows.
- Keep a "streak" going in the Progress tab — small daily sessions beat
  weekend marathons.
