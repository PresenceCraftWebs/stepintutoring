## NSFAS in one page

The **National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS)** funds university and
TVET college study for students from households earning up to R350 000 per
year. It covers tuition, accommodation or transport, food and learning
materials — and it is a *bursary*, not a loan, as long as you pass.

**When to apply:** applications usually open around **September** and close in
**January**. Apply in your matric year — do not wait for your results.

**What you need:**

- Your South African ID (or birth certificate)
- Your parents'/guardian's ID and proof of income (or a SASSA slip)
- Your latest school results

Apply free at [www.nsfas.org.za](https://www.nsfas.org.za). Never pay anyone
to apply for you.

## Other bursaries to look at

- **Funza Lushaka** — full teaching bursary if you want to become a teacher.
- **Provincial government bursaries** — check your province's education site.
- **Company bursaries** (Sasol, Eskom, banks, accounting firms) — usually for
  maths/science/commerce students; most open between March and August of your
  matric year.

## Tips from our tutors

1. Apply to *more than one* bursary — treat it like writing several exams.
2. Keep certified copies of your ID and results ready in one folder.
3. Ask your tutor to check your application before you submit it.
